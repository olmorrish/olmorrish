About

To be completely frank, I'm not sure whether it's unprofessional for someone to write their own description of themselves in first person. Since describing myself in third person would feel rather self-aggrandizing and imply that I'm actually some sort of industry professional, I've opted for more of a jovial letter from a dear friend vibe. With that said...

Hello!

I'm Oliver Morrish, as you've likely guessed. If you're here, I figure you fall neatly into one of three camps: either you are a potential or current employer (in which case, please hire me, that'd be neat), or you know me personally (hello again), or you happen to have the same name as me you were curious about the pretender who bought the URL first.

Either way, it's a pleasure to meet you. Or I imagine it will be, that it was, or that it would be, were it to ever occur. Whatever the case.

You may be interested in my marketable skills, which include a familiarity with the Unity game engine, my fondness for programming, my knowledge of theoretical computer science topics, or my ability write in a technical manner or (in rare cases like this) in a more free-form and creative one.

I'm a In terms of unmarketable skills (heaven forbid), I collect Rubik's cubes, enjoy reading fiction, and I try to play and dissect the design of every genre of video game under the sun. I have a soft spot for video games as a medium, and I honestly believe they have a long way to go in terms of artistic expression, particularly when it comes to narrative experiences and exploring more difficult or nuanced topics.

Projects


The majority of the projects I've worked on can be found on my itch.io, which is linked at the bottom of this page. The majority of my projects are jam games, which, though they made for incredibly fun team-building exercises and learning experiences, are far from my best work. My skills with Unity are best reflected in the VSCS games, the first of which was created for a class project, and the second of which has been in development for a few months, and is much more ambitious.
