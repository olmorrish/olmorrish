About Me

Now, to be completely frank, I'm not sure whether it's unprofessional for someone to write their own description of themselves in first person. Since describing myself in third person would feel rather self-aggrandizing and imply that I'm actually some sort of industry professional, I've opted for more of a jovial-letter-from-a-good-friend sort of vibe. With that said...

Hello!

I'm Oliver Morrish, as you've likely guessed. If you're here, I figure you fall neatly into one of three camps: either you're a potential employer (in which case, you should really hire me, that'd be neay), or you know me personally (hello again), or you happen to have the same name as me you were curious about the pretender who bought the URL first.

Either way, it's a pleasure to meet you. Or I imagine it will be, or that it was, or that it hypothetically would be, were it to ever occur. Whatever the case may be, I hope you're well.

You may be interested in my marketable skills, which include a familiarity with the Unity game engine, my fondness for programming and video games, my knowledge of theoretical computer science topics, or my ability write in a technical manner or (in rare cases like this) in a more free-form and creative one. It may not come as a surprise that I am not, in fact, a web developer.

In terms of unmarketable skills (heaven forbid), I collect Rubik's cubes and I enjoy reading fiction (and occasionally trying to write it). I also try to play and dissect the design of every genre of video game under the sun. I have a soft spot for video games as a medium, and I honestly believe they still have a long way to go in terms of artistic expression, particularly when it comes to narrative experiences and exploring more difficult or nuanced topics.

Projects


The majority of the projects I've worked on can be found on my itch.io, which is linked at the bottom of this page. The majority of my projects are jam games, which, though they made for incredibly fun team-building exercises and learning experiences, are far from my best work. My skills with Unity are best reflected in the VSCS games, the first of which was created for a class project.




The second game, which has been in development for a few months, is much more ambitious. It includes a branching narrative system and dynamic file system, both of which were build and designed by myself. It is currently in the writing stage, with most of the technical systems complete.


My GitHub leans towards school projects, as most of my time in the past two years has been dedicated to learning Unity and trying to learn effective design patterns. Nonetheless, it is still linked below, and there are a few thrown-together ideas on there.
